# Scoring Rubric — Multibagger Hunter (six-pillar / SQGLP)

Read this before scoring. Each pillar is scored **0–100**, then weighted and
summed. **Be stingy.** Most companies should fail this screen — a genuine
multibagger candidate is rare (a few per year, not a few per week). When data is
missing, score the *lower* band; never give the benefit of the doubt.

The math of a multibagger:

> **Return = EPS growth × P/E re-rating (+ dividends)**

Both engines matter, so we score the **earnings engine** (Quality, Growth,
Longevity) and the **re-rating engine** (Size, Price) separately. This mirrors
Motilal Oswal's SQGLP framework and Chris Mayer's *100 Baggers* (median 100x took
~16 years, started small, reinvested at high ROIC, owner-operators).

| Pillar | Weight |
|---|---|
| S — Size & obscurity | 15 |
| Q — Quality of business | 25 |
| G — Growth | 20 |
| L — Longevity / moat | 15 |
| M — Management & capital allocation | 15 |
| P — Price | 10 |

**Total interpretation:** ≥75 = strong candidate (track/accumulate) · 60–74 =
watchlist with specific triggers · 45–59 = interesting but unproven · <45 = pass.
**2+ hard red flags ⇒ AVOID regardless of score.**

---

## S — Size & obscurity (weight 15)

Re-rating needs room. A Rs 80,000 cr company that everyone owns cannot 10x easily;
a well-run, under-followed Rs 1,500 cr one can.

| Condition | Band |
|---|---|
| Mcap Rs 500–5,000 cr | 80–90 |
| Mcap Rs 5,000–10,000 cr | 60–75 |
| Mcap Rs 10,000–15,000 cr | 50–62 |
| Mcap Rs 15,000–50,000 cr | 35–45 |
| Mcap > Rs 50,000 cr | 15–25 |
| Mcap < Rs 500 cr (micro) | cap at 55 — liquidity/governance risk |

Bonus for low institutional holding and thin analyst coverage (room to re-rate).
Penalise if already widely owned/covered.

## Q — Quality of business (weight 25)

The single most important pillar. Quality = high returns on capital that convert
to cash.

| ROCE (sustained) | Base band |
|---|---|
| ≥ 25% | 80–85 |
| 20–25% | 70–78 |
| 15–20% | 55–65 |
| 12–15% | 40–50 |
| < 12% | < 30 |

Adjustments:
- **CFO/EBITDA ≥ 70%** (cumulative 5y): **+8**. **< 50%: −15** (also a hard flag).
- **D/E < 0.3:** +5. **D/E > 1:** −15.
- Gross/operating margin stable or rising: small bonus; falling: penalty.
- Capital-light or improving asset turns: bonus.

## G — Growth (weight 20)

Earnings growth is one of the two return engines, but it must be *profitable*
growth with a visible runway.

| 5y Sales & EPS CAGR (avg) | Band |
|---|---|
| ≥ 20% | 80–85 |
| 15–20% | 60–70 |
| 12–15% | 48–55 |
| 10–12% | 38–45 |
| < 10% | < 35 |

- Cap at **45** if 5y PAT/EPS CAGR < 8% (stalled earnings — no engine).
- Prefer growth backed by operating leverage (margins rising with scale) and a
  long runway (small share of a large/growing TAM).

## L — Longevity / moat (weight 15)

Will the high returns *persist*? Look for an identifiable moat: brand, niche
dominance, switching costs, cost leadership, regulatory barrier, distribution.
Industry tailwind helps; structural decline kills.

Numeric proxy when reading numbers only:

| Condition | Band |
|---|---|
| OPM ≥ 25% **and** ROCE ≥ 20% (durable pricing power) | 68–75 |
| OPM ≥ 18% and ROCE ≥ 18% | 60–68 |
| ROCE ≥ 15% | 50–58 |
| else | 40–48 |

Moat is ultimately qualitative — confirm from annual reports/concalls. Numbers
alone over-rate cyclicals at the top of a cycle.

## M — Management & capital allocation (weight 15)

You are partnering with the promoter for a decade. Integrity and capital
allocation matter more than charisma.

| Promoter holding | Band |
|---|---|
| ≥ 50% and stable/rising | 70–80 |
| 40–50% | 50–58 |
| < 40% | < 38 (also a hard flag) |

Penalties:
- Falling promoter holding (> 3pp) without a stated reason: −10 and cap at 50.
- Promoter pledging > 20% of holding: heavy penalty (also a hard flag).
- High debt, value-destructive M&A, perennial equity dilution: penalty.
- Reward candor in ARs/concalls, rational reinvestment at high ROIC, sensible
  dividend/buyback policy, owner-operator alignment.

## P — Price (weight 10)

Valuation is the smallest weight — a great business at a fair price beats a fair
business at a great price — but never overpay for unproven earnings.

| PEG (P/E ÷ 5y earnings CAGR) | Band |
|---|---|
| ≤ 1.0 | 78–85 |
| 1.0–1.5 | 58–65 |
| 1.5–2.5 | 35–45 |
| > 2.5 | < 25 |

- Hard penalty (≤ 20) for a "story" multiple: P/E > 60 on < 25% growth.
- Sanity-check with earnings yield vs growth; avoid paying for a story.

## Hard red-flag screen (run FIRST)

Run *before* scoring. If **2+ hard flags** fire, stop and issue **AVOID** — no
score compensates for fraud risk.

1. CFO/EBITDA < 50% cumulatively over 5y (profits not converting to cash).
2. Promoter pledging > 20% of promoter holding.
3. Falling promoter holding without stated reason, or promoter stake < 40%.
4. Frequent equity dilution (share-count CAGR > 3–4%).
5. Receivables growing > 1.5× sales growth for 2+ years (channel stuffing).
6. Auditor resignations / small unknown auditor for a large balance sheet /
   frequent auditor changes.
7. Large related-party transactions (loans to promoter entities, group purchases).
8. Contingent liabilities > 25% of net worth.
9. High debt: D/E > 1 (ex-financials) or interest coverage < 3×.
10. Promoter remuneration > 5% of profits in a small company, or a history of
    SEBI actions / insider-trading orders against promoters.

Items 5–8 and 10 generally require reading the annual report (RPT schedule,
contingent-liability note, auditor's report) and are flagged for **manual review**
when only numeric data is available.

---

*Analytical research, not investment advice. Base rates matter: most small caps
underperform. The framework's job is mostly to say **NO**.*
